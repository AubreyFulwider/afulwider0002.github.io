Beryl is a miner that has a high content of beryllium, a very rare metal. This makes beryl a raerly occuring mineral due to the scarcity of beryllium. Beryl is mostly found in granite, rhyolite, metamorphic rocks and in veins and cavities that have had hydrothermic activity.

Calcite is a rock-froming mineral. It is very common and is nearly found anywhere. It is the main mineral in limestone and marble, which make up a huge portion of the Earth's crust. They serve as largest deposit of carbon on our planet if you looked at them as a whole.

Agate is formed in volcanic rock. Namely, the pockets that were one filled with air in lava or magma. As the pockets cooled so did the solutions inside, mostly made up of sillica and impurites. As the solution cooled it would form alternating bands of sillica then impure minerals to create the striped pattern of an agate. The pattern continued until there are no impurites left of the entire solution has cooled.
